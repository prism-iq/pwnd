2008 PLEA DEAL - CONTROVERSIAL AGREEMENT

CASE: State of Florida vs. Jeffrey Epstein
PROSECUTOR: Alexander Acosta (US Attorney, later Trump Labor Secretary)
YEAR: 2008
LOCATION: Palm Beach County, Florida

BACKGROUND:
- Palm Beach Police investigation began 2005
- Over 40 victims identified by police
- Federal investigation recommended 53-page indictment
- Case should have been federal sex trafficking charges

THE DEAL:
- Epstein pleaded guilty to state charges only
- Two counts: solicitation of prostitution, procurement of minor
- Sentence: 18 months county jail (served 13)
- Work release: 6 days a week, 12 hours a day
- Registered as sex offender

CONTROVERSIAL TERMS:
- Federal charges dropped
- Co-conspirators granted immunity (Sarah Kellen, Nadia Marcinkova, etc.)
- Victims not notified (violated Crime Victims Rights Act)
- Sealed from public
- Epstein allowed work release

KEY FIGURES GRANTED IMMUNITY:
- Sarah Kellen
- Nadia Marcinkova
- Adriana Ross
- Lesley Groff

CRITICISM:
- Called "sweetheart deal" by victims
- Judge later ruled victims' rights violated
- Acosta defended: "best outcome given evidence"
- Led to Acosta resignation as Labor Secretary in 2019

AFTERMATH:
- Epstein served at Palm Beach County Stockade
- Allowed to leave for work 6 days/week
- Registered as sex offender Level 3
- 2019: New federal charges filed in SDNY

LEGAL CHALLENGES:
- Julie K. Brown (Miami Herald) investigation exposed deal
- 2019: Judge ruled deal violated victims' rights
- Civil lawsuits followed
- Eventually led to 2019 arrest
