PALM BEACH POLICE INVESTIGATION

LOCATION: Palm Beach, Florida
YEARS: 2005-2006
LEAD DETECTIVE: Joseph Recarey
CHIEF: Michael Reiter

INVESTIGATION ORIGIN:
- March 2005: Mother reported stepdaughter's involvement
- 14-year-old victim came forward
- Palm Beach Police began investigation

KEY FINDINGS:
- Over 40 victims identified
- Pattern of abuse at Palm Beach mansion
- Girls recruited from local high schools
- Paid $200-300 for "massages"
- Victims as young as 14

EVIDENCE COLLECTED:
- Victim testimonies
- Phone records
- Witness statements
- Surveillance evidence
- Message pads with girls' names

DETECTIVE RECAREY'S WORK:
- Interviewed dozens of victims
- Built comprehensive case
- Recommended felony charges
- Frustrated by prosecutorial handling

PALM BEACH MANSION:
- 358 El Brillo Way
- Site of most abuse
- Massage table evidence
- Girls recruited to come here

REFERRAL TO FBI:
- Case referred to FBI 2006
- Federal investigation began
- 53-page federal indictment prepared
- Alexander Acosta handled prosecution

PROSECUTORIAL PROBLEMS:
- Barry Krischer (State Attorney) declined prosecution
- Grand jury only returned single solicitation charge
- Police chief wrote letter of complaint
- Case moved to federal level

CHIEF REITER'S ACTIONS:
- Publicly criticized State Attorney
- Wrote letters demanding action
- Supported victims
- Case became personal mission

OUTCOME:
- Federal plea deal 2008
- 18-month sentence (served 13)
- No federal trafficking charges
- Victims felt betrayed

AFTERMATH:
- Detective Recarey died 2018
- Never saw full justice delivered
- Miami Herald investigation vindicated work
- 2019 charges brought some closure

VICTIMS' STATEMENTS:
- Recruited at school, mall
- Told it was just massage
- Escalated to sexual acts
- Paid to recruit other girls
