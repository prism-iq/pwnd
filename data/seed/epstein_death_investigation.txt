JEFFREY EPSTEIN DEATH - INVESTIGATION SUMMARY

DATE: August 10, 2019
LOCATION: Metropolitan Correctional Center (MCC), New York
OFFICIAL CAUSE: Suicide by hanging
MEDICAL EXAMINER: Dr. Barbara Sampson

CIRCUMSTANCES:
- Found unresponsive in cell at 6:30 AM
- Pronounced dead at New York Presbyterian Hospital
- Was awaiting trial on federal sex trafficking charges
- Had been on suicide watch, then removed

TIMELINE OF EVENTS:
- July 6, 2019: Arrested at Teterboro Airport
- July 8, 2019: Denied bail
- July 23, 2019: Found semi-conscious with marks on neck
- July 24-29, 2019: Placed on suicide watch
- July 29, 2019: Removed from suicide watch
- August 10, 2019: Found dead

SECURITY FAILURES:
- Guards did not check cell for 8 hours
- Two guards allegedly sleeping, falsified records
- Security cameras malfunctioned or had no footage
- Cellmate transferred night before death
- Bedsheets not suicide-proof as required

AUTOPSY FINDINGS:
- Multiple neck bone fractures
- Hyoid bone broken (unusual for hanging suicide)
- Dr. Michael Baden (hired by family): consistent with homicide
- Official ruling: suicide

CONSPIRACY THEORIES:
- "Epstein didn't kill himself" became viral meme
- Questions about powerful people he could implicate
- Timing convenient for those named in documents
- Similar circumstances to Jean-Luc Brunel death

GUARDS CHARGED:
- Tova Noel and Michael Thomas
- Charged with falsifying records
- Admitted to not checking on Epstein
- Deferred prosecution agreement reached

INVESTIGATIONS:
- FBI investigation ongoing
- DOJ Inspector General review
- No charges for anyone else
- MCC warden reassigned

KEY QUESTIONS UNANSWERED:
- Why was suicide watch removed?
- Why were cameras not functioning?
- Who authorized cellmate removal?
- Why were guards not monitoring?
